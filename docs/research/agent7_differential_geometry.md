### Relevance to Continuous Geometric Analysis

The research suggests a transition from discrete, heuristic-based process mining to a continuous, manifold-based approach. 
- **Differential Geometry**: As evidenced in internal project reports (e.g., `BLUE_OCEAN_INNOVATION_THESIS.md`), the Abstract Syntax Tree (AST) and process state spaces are increasingly treated as continuous manifolds where changes are represented as differential perturbations.
- **Information Geometry & Fisher-Rao**: These concepts are critical for defining a "distance" between process models. The Fisher-Rao metric allows for the quantification of information loss when simplifying a complex process model, providing a rigorous mathematical basis for the fitness/precision trade-off.
- **Convergence**: Stochastic convergence in machine learning models of processes is viewed as a trajectory on these manifolds, where the goal is to reach a stable state that maximizes information gain while minimizing complexity.
